Hello. Come in. Sit down. Yes, over there. No, the other chair. That is where the cat sits, but the cat is not here right now. Or maybe the cat died. I don't remember. But sit down.

I have something very important to tell you today. It is about the water. And the frogs. Please don't touch that radio, the knobs fall off. Thank you.

The water is different now. I used to drink the water from the tap when I was a girl. We had a sink in the kitchen. A white sink. My mother, she had blue eyes, she used to wash the potatoes in that sink. The water was clear then. Clear like glass. Now the water is heavy. It sits in the glass and it doesn't move right. It has things in it.

Chemicals. That is a big word. The doctor said chemicals. He had a red tie. Or maybe that was my son. My son wears ties now. He grew up. He used to be small, you know. He used to fit in the basket by the stove. Now he brings me pills in a little plastic cup. Monday is the yellow one. Tuesday is the white one. I think the chemicals are in the pills too. But I am talking about the water.

The water has chemicals and the chemicals change things. They change the culture.

Culture. What is culture? It used to be the choir. We sang in the church on Thursdays. Mrs. Hansen played the piano. Her fingers were fat but they moved fast. Now the culture is the television. The television is very loud. The people on the television move too fast. Their mouths go up and down but the words don't match their faces. It is because of the water. The water makes everything go too fast or too slow. Time is wet now. Do you understand? Time used to be dry, like a clock ticking. Now time is wet like a sponge.

And the frogs. Oh, the frogs. I must tell you about the frogs.

Last Tuesday. Or maybe it was the winter of 1964. I was walking by the pond. The pond is gone now. They built a parking lot. But when the pond was there, there were frogs. Green frogs. They sat on the lily pads. Lily pads are round. Like plates.

But now the frogs are different. I read it in the paper. The paper was on the table, but someone moved it. The frogs are changing because of the water. The chemicals in the water go into the pond and the frogs drink it. The boy frogs are becoming girl frogs. Or the girl frogs are becoming boy frogs. I can't keep it straight. Everything is mixed up now. Just like the laundry. I put the whites in with the colors and now everything is pink. The frogs are pink. No, green. Mixed up.

If the frogs are mixed up, then the people are mixed up too. That is how culture changes. It starts with the water, then the frogs, then the men in the suits on the television, and then us.

I went to the store yesterday. Or maybe it was a dream. I had a list. The list had butter and milk and bread. But the store didn't have butter. They had things in plastic containers. The man at the counter, he had very soft hands. He smiled at me, but his eyes were looking at the ceiling. Why was he looking at the ceiling? Because the water changed him. The chemicals made his hands soft. Men used to have rough hands. My husband, Arthur, he fixed the roof. He had hands like sandpaper. Now the men have soft hands and they look at the ceiling. That is the culture changing. Do you see?

It is in the music too. The music on the radio. It used to have a beat. Boom-boom. A heart beat. Now the music is just a hum. Like the refrigerator. The refrigerator hums all night. I think the refrigerator is full of chemicals. The music makes the young people sway. They don't dance like we danced. We danced in circles. Now they just sway like the frogs in the water. Swaying. Waiting for a fly to go by.

My daughter came to visit. She put her coat on the bed. I told her not to put the coat on the bed, but she did it anyway. She always does what she wants. She talked about her job. She works with computers. Computers are just glass and water, if you think about it. Little puddles inside a box. She doesn't know she is drinking the chemicals. I wanted to tell her. I said, "Don't drink the water." She said, "Mom, it's bottled water." But I know the bottles are just frogs in disguise.

No. That is silly. The bottles are plastic. But the plastic leaks into the water. The doctor told me. The man with the red tie. He said the plastic leaks. So the bottled water is worse. It is a circle. A frog circle.

Culture is a circle too. We used to sit on the porch. The neighbors would walk by. Mr. Johnson would wave. He had a wooden leg. Thump, thump, thump. Now nobody sits on the porch. The porches are gone. The houses are too close together. People look at their phones. The phones are glowing frogs. They hold them in their soft hands and they tap, tap, tap. They are drinking the glow. The glow is a chemical. It changes the brain. The brain becomes like a lily pad. Floating. Not attached to the dirt.

The dirt is important. I had a garden. Peas and carrots. You have to put your hands in the dirt to grow carrots. Now the carrots come in bags. They are already clean. They have no dirt on them. If there is no dirt, how do we know they are real? The chemicals wash the dirt off the culture. Everything is clean and smooth and soft. Like the man at the store.

The frogs know. I saw one on the window sill last night. It was raining. The rain is chemical water falling from the sky. The frog just sat there. He was looking at me. He had big eyes. Bigger than Mrs. Hansen's eyes. He didn't ribbit. He just looked. He was waiting for me to turn into a frog too.

I am turning into a frog. My skin feels loose. It used to fit tight over my bones. Now it hangs. It is wet skin. My knees hurt when I stand up. That is because my bones are turning into jelly. Jelly is like water. It is the chemicals settling in my knees.

When I was young, we were solid. We were trees. Now we are jellyfish. Floating in the chemical water. Bumping into each other but not saying sorry. Culture used to be saying sorry when you bump into someone. Now it is just floating past.

Arthur used to smoke a pipe. The smoke smelled like cherries. Cherry smoke is not a chemical. It is a tree. Trees don't have chemicals. They drink the rain, but maybe the rain was clean then. Before the frogs changed.

I am tired now. The TV is too loud. Can you turn it off? No, leave it. If I turn it off, the room will be too quiet and I will hear the frogs in the walls. There are frogs in the walls. They are humming. The refrigerator is humming too. They are the same sound.

Everything is the same sound now. Everything is water. Don't drink the water. Put the coat on the chair, not the bed. Where is the cat? Did you see the cat? The cat fell in the water. The cat is a frog now.

Just close the door when you leave. The chemicals are trying to get out.
